# Team Leadership

## Introduction
Team leadership in software engineering combines technical judgment, communication, and people management to deliver results.

## Problem Statement
Strong technical leadership is not only about writing code; it is also about aligning teams, resolving conflict, and making decisions under uncertainty.

## Why this exists
Senior and staff engineers must influence outcomes, mentor others, and keep teams moving while maintaining engineering quality.

## Real-world analogy
A conductor leads an orchestra by coordinating diverse musicians so the performance is cohesive and expressive.

## Definition
Team leadership is the ability to guide a technical team through planning, execution, and delivery while balancing strategy, people, and engineering practices.

## Key concepts
- **Vision and alignment**
- **Decision-making**
- **Feedback and coaching**
- **Stakeholder management**
- **Psychological safety**

## Leadership behaviors
1. Set clear goals and success criteria.
2. Communicate trade-offs and risk transparently.
3. Mentor engineers through technical and career growth.
4. Create a safe environment for feedback and experimentation.

## Interview questions
### Beginner
- How do you prioritize work as a team leader?
- Answer: Align on business impact, technical risk, and team capacity.

### Intermediate
- How do you give negative feedback effectively?
- Answer: Focus on specific behavior, describe impact, and suggest improvement.

### Senior
- How do you handle conflicting stakeholder priorities?
- Answer: Clarify goals, communicate trade-offs, and escalate with evidence.

### Staff Engineer
- How do you build engineering trust across teams?
- Answer: Deliver consistently, document decisions, and involve stakeholders early.

## Common leadership challenges
- Allowing technical debt to build without mitigation.
- Overloading the team with context switches.
- Avoiding hard conversations on scope or quality.

## Best practices
- Use clear decision records and architecture docs.
- Pair technical planning with strong interpersonal context.
- Support team autonomy while removing blockers.

## When NOT to use a top-down style
- When the team needs ownership and empowerment.
- When you want better collaboration and innovation.
