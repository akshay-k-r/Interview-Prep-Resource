# Communication

## Introduction
Effective communication is essential for engineering leaders to align teams and drive execution.

## Problem Statement
Miscommunication causes delays, rework, and confusion across teams and stakeholders.

## Why this exists
Technical work is collaborative, and clarity in written and verbal communication reduces friction.

## Real-world analogy
A well-written blueprint helps builders align on a complex construction project.

## Definition
Communication is the process of sharing context, decisions, and expectations clearly with peers, managers, and stakeholders.

## Key concepts
- **Clarity**
- **Audience awareness**
- **Active listening**
- **Documentation**
- **Follow-up**

## Best practices
- Use concise language and avoid jargon when writing for non-technical stakeholders.
- Confirm understanding through summaries and check-ins.
- Document decisions and assumptions to reduce ambiguity.

## Interview questions
### Beginner
- How do you communicate technical changes to non-technical stakeholders?
- Answer: Explain the impact, timeline, and what will change in simple terms.

### Intermediate
- How do you improve communication in a remote team?
- Answer: Use written updates, synchronous meetings for alignment, and shared dashboards.

### Senior
- How do you handle a communication breakdown on your team?
- Answer: Identify the root cause, clarify expectations, and create better feedback loops.

### Staff Engineer
- How do you influence cross-team alignment on a platform decision?
- Answer: Collect viewpoints, document trade-offs, and present a clear recommendation with risks.
