# Stakeholder Management

## Introduction
Stakeholder management helps engineering leaders keep business partners, customers, and leadership aligned.

## Problem Statement
Without active stakeholder management, projects can drift, expectations can misalign, and delivery can suffer.

## Why this exists
Engineering work is delivered in the context of business priorities and customer needs.

## Real-world analogy
A project manager keeps sponsors informed and involved while the team builds the product.

## Definition
Stakeholder management is the process of understanding stakeholder needs, communicating trade-offs, and aligning plans.

## Key concepts
- **Stakeholder map**
- **Expectation setting**
- **Decision transparency**
- **Feedback loops**
- **Risk communication**

## Best practices
- Identify stakeholders early and understand their goals.
- Communicate progress and risks proactively.
- Use scheduled checkpoints for alignment.

## Interview questions
### Beginner
- What is stakeholder management?
- Answer: Keeping key people informed and aligned on project outcomes.

### Intermediate
- How do you manage conflicting stakeholder expectations?
- Answer: Clarify priorities, escalate trade-offs, and recommend a balanced plan.

### Senior
- How do you use data when communicating with stakeholders?
- Answer: Use metrics and evidence to make trade-offs explicit and reduce subjective debate.
