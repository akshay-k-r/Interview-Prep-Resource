# Decision Making

## Introduction
Effective decision making is a core skill for engineering leaders and senior technical contributors.

## Problem Statement
Poor decisions can waste resources, create technical debt, and harm team morale.

## Why this exists
Technical leaders must choose between trade-offs under uncertainty and incomplete information.

## Real-world analogy
A chess player chooses the best move by balancing immediate gain and future position.

## Definition
Decision making is the process of evaluating options, weighing trade-offs, and committing to a direction.

## Key concepts
- **Trade-offs**
- **Cost of delay**
- **Decisiveness**
- **Data-informed judgment**
- **Post-mortem learning**

## Best practices
- Frame decisions with clear criteria and constraints.
- Involve the right stakeholders and subject matter experts.
- Make decisions quickly when the cost of waiting exceeds the benefit.

## Interview questions
### Beginner
- What information should you gather before a technical decision?
- Answer: Goals, constraints, risks, and alternatives.

### Intermediate
- How do you avoid analysis paralysis?
- Answer: Pick the smallest decision that moves you forward and validate assumptions early.

### Senior
- How do you handle a decision that later proves wrong?
- Answer: Acknowledge it, learn from it, and course-correct quickly.
